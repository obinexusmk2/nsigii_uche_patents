OBINexus is a visionary initiative founded by Nnamdi Michael Okpala on November 17, 2024, to address systemic failures in social care, housing, and institutional accountability through a unified framework of advanced technology, sustainable housing, and computational justice. This 5-year plan outlines a strategic roadmap to mitigate civil collapse by transitioning individuals from social care to independence, building phenomenological person-to-person economic and housing systems, and establishing OBINexus as a global leader in trauma-informed engineering and consciousness-preserving architectures.The plan addresses a £140 million legal claim against systemic institutional failures, including prolonged homelessness, mental health neglect, and violations of human rights, as evidenced by the Romford Court case scheduled for August 31, 2025. The initiative aims to create sustainable communities (8,100 homes on 90 acres) under the Uch'e Division and deploy advanced computing frameworks through the OBINexus Computing Division, integrating quantum research, AI-driven entrapment detection, and legal technology to enforce accountability and protect vulnerable populations.\section*{Background and Context}
